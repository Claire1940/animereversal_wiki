## Understanding the Fool Mask Mechanic

The **anime reversal fool mask** acts as a chaotic catalyst within the game's upgrade system. Unlike standard reroll crystals, the Mask doesn't just change a single stat; it has the potential to swap entire sets of blessings or traits while retaining their current enhancement levels. This makes it a double-edged sword that can either propel a unit to godhood or leave a player scrambling to recover their lost utility.

### Core Functionality in 2026

When activated, the Mask targets the unit's current traits and "reverses" their logic or swaps them for random alternatives of equal or higher rarity. This is particularly useful when you have a high-level enhancement on a mediocre trait like "Basic" or "Silencer" and want to gamble for the elusive 0.1% Elite trait without losing your investment in enhancement materials.

| Feature | Effect | Strategic Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Trait Swapping** | Randomly replaces all current traits | High (for bypassing bad RNG) |
| **Enhancement Retention** | Keeps the level of the previous trait | Essential for late-game scaling |
| **Rarity Up-grade** | Small chance to jump from A-tier to SSS-tier | The primary reason for usage |
| **Cost** | 180 - 300 Cosmic Fragments/Gems | Moderate investment |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never use the Mask on a unit that already possesses an **Elite** or **Doom** trait. The risk of losing a 0.1% rarity roll for a random swap is statistically unfavorable in the 2026 meta.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the anime reversal fool mask work on evolved units?**

A: Yes, the Mask can be used on any unit regardless of their evolution stage. In fact, it is often better to use it on evolved units because their base stats are higher, making the trait multipliers even more effective.

**Q: What happens if I use the Mask and get a worse trait?**

A: Unfortunately, the swap is final. This is why the **anime reversal fool mask** is considered a high-risk item. Always ensure you have backup rerolls before using one on a critical unit.

**Q: Where can I find Megaphones for the Qin Sha trade?**

A: Megaphones are rare drops found exclusively in Portals Tier 5 and above. They have a roughly 5% drop rate from the final boss of the portal.

**Q: Is the Elite trait really worth 1,000 rerolls?**

A: For competitive play, yes. A 250% damage boost is the difference between clearing a Nightmare stage and failing at the final wave. However, for casual play, a Lunar or Doom trait is more than sufficient.
