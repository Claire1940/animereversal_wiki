## How to Farm the Green Chip Effectively

Farming the **Anime Reversal（Roblox 游戏） green chip** requires a focus on high-difficulty content. While Story Mode provides a decent introduction to the game's mechanics, it is rarely the optimal place for rare material drops. To maximize your hourly yield, you should focus on Portals and Infinite Mode.

### Tier 5 to Tier 8 Portals
Portals are the most reliable source for the **Anime Reversal（Roblox 游戏） green chip**. As of the 0.5 update in 2026, the drop rates have been adjusted to reward players who tackle Tier 5 and above. 

1.  **Tier 5-6:** Offers a 15% drop chance per clear.
2.  **Tier 7-8:** Offers a 35% drop chance, with a high probability of dropping multiple chips if you achieve a "Perfect" clear rating.
3.  **Nightmare Difficulty:** Playing portals on Nightmare mode adds a 1.2x multiplier to all material drops, including chips and megaphones.

### Infinite Mode Milestones
In Infinite Mode, chips are rewarded at specific wave milestones. Reaching Wave 50, 75, and 100 provides a guaranteed cache of materials. This is often preferred by players who have strong "Elite" units like Red Hair Awaken, as they can AFK through the earlier waves with minimal placement.

## FAQ

**Q: Where is the best place to farm the Anime Reversal（Roblox 游戏） green chip?**

A: The most efficient farming method is running Tier 7 or Tier 8 Portals on Nightmare difficulty. These have the highest drop rates and often reward multiple chips per run compared to lower-tier content.

**Q: Why can't I see the Qin Sha NPC in my portal?**

A: Qin Sha only spawns between waves 7 and 13. If you reach wave 14, he will despawn. Additionally, he has a roughly 10% spawn chance, so you may need to run several portals before he appears.

**Q: Do I lose my traits when I evolve a unit to Secret status?**

A: No, your traits and stats carry over during the evolution process. However, it is highly recommended to roll for a "Meta" trait (like Elite or Godly) before evolving, as the unit's power spike is much more noticeable with high-tier passives.

**Q: What is the "World CD" mentioned in unit abilities?**

A: World CD stands for World Cooldown. This means that if you have multiple units of the same type (e.g., two Awakened Shanks), using the active ability on one will put the ability on cooldown for all others on the map. This prevents players from infinitely stunning bosses.
