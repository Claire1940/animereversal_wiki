## The Quest for Twilight: Finding the Firecracker

To begin the journey toward obtaining the best support in the game, you must first locate the Twilight NPC. Twilight offers a "Special Quest" that requires several rare materials, the most difficult of which is the **anime reversal firecracker**. 

Unlike standard evolution items that can be purchased directly from the main shop with common currency, the firecracker is tied to RNG and active gameplay. You cannot simply buy your way through this first phase unless you have already put in the hours of grinding required for the secondary materials.

### Quest Requirements Overview

| Item Required | Quantity | Acquisition Method |

## How to Obtain the Anime Reversal Firecracker

The **anime reversal firecracker** is notoriously difficult to find due to its low 0.5% drop probability. This item drops randomly while playing through various game modes. There is no specific "secret stage" that guarantees a drop, though efficiency is key. Most veteran players in 2026 recommend farming Namek on Easy or Normal difficulty to maximize the number of completions per hour.

Because the firecracker is a prerequisite for the Twilight quest, you should prioritize this before spending your gems or crystals. There is nothing worse than having the currency ready but being stuck behind the RNG of the drop. Once the firecracker is in your inventory, you can proceed to the NPC to turn in the quest and receive the base Twilight unit.

## Evolving Twilight into Twilight Mirage

Obtaining the base unit is only the first half of the battle. To truly unlock the potential of the **anime reversal firecracker** quest, you must evolve Twilight into her "Mirage" form. This evolution significantly boosts her passive abilities and transforms her from a niche support into a mandatory team member for endgame content.

### Evolution Requirements

To evolve Twilight, you must meet the following criteria:
1. **Level Requirement:** The unit should ideally be Level 50+.
2. **Takedowns:** You must accumulate **1,000 takedowns** with Twilight equipped in your active party.
3. **Evolution Materials:** While the base quest required the firecracker, the evolution itself focuses on unit experience and kill count.

| Evolution Stage | Requirement | Reward/Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base Form** | Twilight | Standard Support |
| **Kill Grind** | 1,000 Takedowns | Evolution Eligibility |
| **Final Form** | **Twilight Mirage** | Elite Support + New Passives |

## Twilight Mirage: Passive Abilities and Meta Utility

Once you have utilized the **anime reversal firecracker** to secure Twilight Mirage, you gain access to some of the most "broken" passives in the game. Her kit is designed to amplify the damage of your DPS units, specifically those that rely on fire elements or critical hits.

### Trickster Marks
Every attack Twilight Mirage performs applies a "Trickster Mark" to the enemy. When a marked enemy is hit by any of your other units, there is a **2% to 10% chance** to trigger additional damage based on the initial attack's power. After the explosion, the mark disappears and must be reapplied.

### Blazing Performance
This is a massive boon for fire-heavy teams. All fire element units in your range gain a **25% increase in total damage**. In the current 2026 meta, pairing her with units like Shanks or other high-tier fire DPS makes clearing Tier 8 portals significantly easier.

### Grand Finale (Max Upgrade)
When Twilight Mirage reaches her maximum upgrade level on the field, all units on the map receive a global buff:
* **+20% Critical Rate**
* **+50% Critical Damage**

> **⚠️ Warning:** Twilight Mirage has a 4-placement limit. It is generally recommended to keep one at max upgrade for the map-wide crit buffs, while the others can be kept at Upgrade 5 for the "Trickster Play" Full AoE coverage.

## Optimized Placement and Strategy

Since Twilight Mirage is a ground unit with a large range, placement is flexible but critical. To maximize the application of Trickster Marks, you should place her at the beginning of the track where she can hit the most enemies frequently.

| Upgrade Level | Ability Name | Attack Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Upgrade 1-4** | Standard Attack | Small AoE / Single Target |
| **Upgrade 5** | **Trickster Play** | **Full AoE** |
| **Upgrade 9** | Toy Destruction | Medium AoE (High Damage) |

Most players prefer to stop at Upgrade 5 for three of their placements to maintain the Full AoE status. This ensures that every enemy walking down the path is constantly marked, allowing your main DPS to trigger the additional damage from Trickster Marks more often. The fourth placement should be maxed out to trigger the **Grand Finale** global buff.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to get the anime reversal firecracker?**

A: The fastest way is to clear low-level stages like Namek on Easy. Since the drop rate is a flat 0.5% per completion, speed is more important than difficulty. You want to maximize the number of games finished per hour.

**Q: Can I trade the anime reversal firecracker with other players?**

A: No, the firecracker is a quest-bound item and cannot be traded. You must farm it on your own account to satisfy the NPC Twilight's requirements.

**Q: Does the firecracker drop in AFK mode?**

A: No, the firecracker typically requires active play completions. While you can earn gems and experience in AFK mode, special quest items like the **anime reversal firecracker** usually require stage clears.

**Q: Is it worth using a Lead trait on Twilight Mirage?**

A: Generally, no. Since you can place four of her, a Lead trait is better spent on a primary DPS unit. Twilight Mirage provides her best buffs simply by being on the map and reaching max upgrade.
