## Understanding the Trait System in Anime Reversal

Traits are randomized modifiers applied to units upon summoning or through the reroll system. In the current 2026 meta, the difference between a unit with no trait and one with a top-tier "Elite" trait is staggering. For example, a level 50 Igris might have a base damage of roughly 65,000, but with the right trait, that number can skyrocket toward 200,000.

The game utilizes a ranking system for these traits, often tied to the unit's potential stats, which range up to Z+. While the specific percentage boosts can vary, the goal is always to aim for traits that multiply your raw damage output or provide utility that complements the unit's unique abilities.

## Best Units to Prioritize for Trait Rerolls

Not every unit deserves your hard-earned trait rolls. In the current meta, you should focus your resources on "Meta" and "Secret" tier units. These units have higher base scaling, meaning the percentage boost from the **anime reversal best traits** provides a much larger numerical increase.

### Ruda (The DPS King)
Ruda is widely considered the best unit for soloing content. With an Elite trait and a high attack stat (Z+), Ruda can reach damage numbers exceeding 200,000. If you have Ruda, he should be the first unit you attempt to roll an Elite trait for.

### Shanks (The Hybrid Support/DPS)
Shanks is an S-tier unit because he provides both massive damage and a stun every 8 seconds. Giving Shanks a trait that boosts his speed or damage makes him an indispensable part of any Tier 8 portal team.

### Igris & Jin Wu (The Duo)
Igris has some of the highest base damage in the game (around 65,000 at level 50). When paired with Jin Wu, Igris deals 30% more damage. Applying one of the **anime reversal best traits** to Igris while this synergy is active creates a DPS monster that can shred bosses in seconds.

## Advanced Trait Strategies for 2026

As you progress into the late game, simply having a "Good" trait isn't enough. You must look at how traits interact with unit passives.

* **Burn Intensity:** Units like Ace and Jen rely on burn damage. Traits that increase raw attack power also increase the "tick" damage of their flames.
* **Wave Scaling:** Some units, like Goku, gain damage every few waves. While Goku is currently niche due to the 15-wave limit in most modes, he becomes much more viable in Infinite Mode when paired with a damage-stacking trait.
* **Cool-down Thresholds:** Certain units have abilities that trigger based on their cooldown length. Enjo, for instance, deals 50% more damage if his cooldown is longer than 7 seconds. Be careful not to use a speed trait that accidentally lowers his cooldown below this threshold, or you will lose his massive passive bonus.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the anime reversal best traits for beginners?**

A: If you are just starting, any trait that provides a flat "Attack %" boost is great. However, as you reach the mid-game, you should save your Trait Rolls specifically to hunt for the **Elite** trait on your Mythic units.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm Trait Rolls?**

A: The Chamber is the most efficient location. You can AFK in the Chamber to accumulate rolls over time. While it was recently nerfed, it still provides enough currency to get at least one unit to "Elite" status every day or two if you are consistent.

**Q: Does the NPC Qin Sha always spawn?**

A: No, he has a low spawn chance (around 10%). He only appears in Tier 5 portals or higher, specifically between waves 7 and 13. You must have 5,000 Gems, a Megaphone, and 30 Trait Rolls ready to trade with him for a Premium Ticket.

**Q: Can I have multiple traits on one unit?**

A: No, each unit can only hold one trait at a time. When you reroll, the new trait will replace the old one, so be careful not to roll over a high-tier trait like Elite unless you are absolutely sure.
