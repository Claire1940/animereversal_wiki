## Deep Dive: S-Tier Meta Definining Units

The S-Tier is reserved for units that are practically mandatory for clearing end-game content. These units either provide unparalleled damage or offer buffs that cannot be replicated by lower-tier alternatives.

### Kaida (Battle Pass Exclusive)
Kaida has quickly risen to the top of the **anime reverse tier list** due to a passive ability that makes enemies take 20% more damage from all sources permanently. When combined with her Damage Over Time (DOT) effects, she becomes a force multiplier for any team composition. ### Egress and Jinmu Synergy
Egress remains the king of DPS, but his true potential is unlocked when paired with Jinmu. The team-up bonus provided by Jinmu elevates Egress from a strong attacker to a boss-shredding monster. This duo is currently the gold standard for high-wave survival and speed-running dungeons.

## The Ragnarok Units: Saka, Quincy, and Hadtas

The Ragnarok update brought several new faces to the roster. While some have integrated perfectly into the meta, others serve as mid-tier fillers for players who haven't yet pulled the secret units.

*   **Saka:** The standout of the group. His immunity to stun is a game-changer in specific maps where CC (Crowd Control) is prevalent. His ability to increase his own attack speed and damage over time makes him a self-sufficient powerhouse.
*   **Quincy:** A reliable unit that performs well in most scenarios. However, in the context of a 2026 **anime reverse tier list**, he sits firmly in B-Tier because he lacks the specialized utility found in S-Tier supports.
*   **Hadtas:** Generally considered the weakest of the trio. While his raw damage stats are acceptable, his passive is underwhelming compared to the current powerhouses.

## Understanding Hill Units and Situational Picks

Hill units like Yoru and Ichigo often find themselves lower on general tier lists, but this is largely due to map design rather than unit stats. Currently, very few maps offer the "Hill" placement necessary to utilize these units effectively.

| Unit | Passive Ability | Strategic Use |

| **Ichigo** | 15% Dmg increase on same target | Ideal for focused fire on single-target bosses. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid over-investing in hill units unless you specifically plan to run the few maps that support them. Their situational nature makes them a poor choice for a general-purpose team.

## Analyzing the Secret Units: Jack and Ble

Secret units are often expected to be the pinnacle of power, but the 1.0 update has sparked some debate regarding Jack and Ble. Jack, in particular, features a passive that increases his SPA (Seconds Per Attack) when more than two enemies are in range. Since a higher SPA means a slower attack rate, this acts as a functional nerf in high-density waves. 

Ble, the first secret hill unit, boasts impressive damage (up to 65,000 DPS), but the requirement to stack marks three times before the 100% damage bonus kicks in is often redundant. In the current meta, most enemies are dead before the third mark can even be applied. For these reasons, they occupy the lower tiers despite their rarity.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best unit in the anime reverse tier list for beginners?**

A: Ruda remains the best unit for beginners. He is relatively easy to obtain compared to secret units, has a manageable cost, and provides consistent damage that scales well into the mid-game.

**Q: Why is Thor ranked so low despite being rare?**

A: Thor is primarily a collector's item in 2026. His 0.2% drop rate makes him difficult to get, but his passive relies heavily on critical hits. Without a guaranteed crit rate, his performance is too inconsistent for high-level play.

**Q: Are hill units worth using in the current 1.0 update?**

A: Generally, no. While units like Ble and Yoru have high stats, the lack of hill placements on most maps makes them difficult to deploy. Stick to ground units for a more versatile roster.

**Q: How does Kaida's permanent damage buff work?**

A: Kaida applies a debuff to enemies that increases the damage they take from all sources by 20%. Unlike temporary buffs, this effect stays on the target, making it incredibly powerful for taking down high-health bosses in raids and infinite modes.
