## Detailed Breakdown of Top Tier Units

### Kaida (Battle Pass Exclusive)
Kaida has quickly risen to the top of the **anime reversal tier list** following the 1.0 update. As a support-DPS hybrid, she applies a permanent debuff to enemies, causing them to take 20% more damage from all sources. When paired with high-damage carries, this multiplier becomes the difference between clearing a wave and being overwhelmed.

### Shanks (Secret)
Obtaining Shanks is a complex process involving a specific evolution method and an NPC spawn, but the effort is well worth it. His ability to stun enemies consistently makes him the premier crowd control unit in the game. In the current 2026 meta, stalling fast-moving bosses is the only way to ensure your heavy hitters can finish the job.

### Egress & Jin Wu Synergy
While Egress boasts the highest base damage in the game (approximately 65,000 at level 50), his true potential is unlocked through his "Team Up" passive. When Jin Wu is present on the field, Egress deals 30% more damage and gains a bonus against boss-type enemies. This duo is a staple for any player looking to maximize their damage per second (DPS).

## High Tier Units (A-Tier)

A-Tier units are incredibly strong and can carry a player through most content. They generally lack the game-breaking utility of S-Tier units but remain highly efficient for their cost.

* **Saka:** A standout from the Ragnarok update. Saka is immune to stuns and features a passive that increases damage over time (DOT) while stacking attack speed.
* **Ace:** A reliable burn unit with high area-of-effect (AoE) damage. Ace is perfect for clearing large groups of minor enemies before they reach your defenses.
* **Kyito:** While technically meta-level in terms of stats, Kyito is placed in A-Tier due to the extreme difficulty of obtaining him. His rarity makes him less accessible for the general player base.
* **Rio:** A solid sub-meta support unit that provides a 50% damage boost when enemies fall below 30% HP.

## Mid Tier and Situational Units (B & C Tier)

These units are often outclassed by the meta but can serve as functional placeholders or niche counters for specific maps.

| Unit | Tier | Reason for Placement |
|---|---|---|

| **Quincy** | **B** | Decent stats but outshined by Saka in the same category. |
| **Ichigo** | **B** | Strong damage on single targets, but **hill placement** limits his utility. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be cautious when using Jack. His current passive mechanics suggest an increase in SPA (Seconds Per Attack), which actually slows down his firing rate. Until this is patched or clarified, he remains a risky investment.

## Units to Avoid (D-Tier)

The following units are generally considered "collectors' items." They have low drop rates or high costs but provide very little value in actual gameplay compared to easier-to-obtain Mythics.

1. **Thor:** Despite his 0.2% drop rate, his passive relies on a low critical hit chance and provides minimal utility.
2. **Eisen:** Often described as a "worse Ruda," Eisen gains a small damage buff per kill, but the cap is too low to compete with top-tier carries.
3. **Boti:** Her ability requires 15 attacks to trigger a single 200% damage hit with a 5-second cooldown, making her DPS output significantly lower than standard Mythics.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best overall unit in the anime reversal tier list?**

A: Currently, **Kaida** is considered the best overall unit due to her universal 20% damage buff and DOT capabilities. For raw damage, **Ruda** remains the king of solo clears.

**Q: Why is Goku ranked lower than expected?**

A: Goku's passive provides a 4% damage increase every three waves. Since most standard game modes in 2026 are only 15 waves long, he never reaches his full potential compared to units with flat buffs or better scaling.

**Q: Are hill units viable in the 1.0 update?**

A: Hill units like Ichigo and Yoru are situational. While their damage is decent, most maps have poor hill placement, making ground-based units more reliable for consistent defense.

**Q: How do I get the Shanks secret unit?**

A: Shanks requires a specific evolution process followed by waiting for a rare NPC to spawn in the game world. It is one of the most time-consuming units to obtain but offers the best crowd control in the game.
