## Evolution Requirements and Stages

Evolving Shanks is a two-step process. You cannot jump directly from the base form to the Elite form. You must first progress through the intermediate evolution before applying the secret item. Fortunately, Anime Reversal does not currently impose a kill requirement for the final stage of this specific evolution, making it one of the more accessible "Secret" upgrades if you have the necessary currency.

### Step 1: The First Evolution
The first evolution brings Shanks to his second form. While this version is an improvement over the base unit, its stats are generally considered "average" compared to other high-tier units. However, it is a mandatory bridge to the final form. You will need standard evolution materials (often referred to as "Red Materials") which can be farmed in various challenge modes.

### Step 2: The Elite Evolution (The Hat)
To reach the final form, you need the **Straw Hat** (or Red Hair's Hat) item purchased from the Shanks NPC. Once you have this item in your inventory, you can navigate to the Evolution menu, select your second-form Shanks, and apply the item.

| Evolution Stage | Item Required | Kill Requirement | Key Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Base to Form 2** | Standard Red Materials | None | Improved Range/Damage |
| **Form 2 to Elite** | **Red Hair's Hat** | **None** | Massive AOE & Stun Skill |

## Shanks Unit Analysis and Stats

Once you have successfully completed the process of **how to evolve shanks in anime reversal**, you will possess one of the most versatile units in the game. His Elite form is characterized by a "Full AOE" attack pattern at max level, meaning he can clear entire waves of enemies simultaneously.

The Elite Shanks features a unique passive called "Conqueror's Spirit." This passive gives every attack a **20% chance to deal 1.5x damage**. When combined with his high base damage (reaching nearly 200,000 at max upgrade), this crit-like mechanic allows him to shred boss health bars with ease.

### Aura and Skill Mechanics
Unlike many other towers that only deal damage at their point of impact, Shanks features an "Aura Damage" mechanic. Enemies that get closer to the unit take increased damage. Furthermore, his active skill provides a reliable **stun**, which is essential for stopping fast-moving "Runner" enemies or delaying bosses during critical waves.
