## Overview of Story Mode Chapters

The campaign in Anime Reversal is currently divided into three distinct thematic chapters, each inspired by legendary anime locations. Each chapter serves as a gateway to specific evolution materials and currency farming opportunities.

### Chapter 1: Plant Name
As the introductory chapter, Plant Name focuses on teaching players the basics of placement and wave management. While it starts easy, the Nightmare difficulty setting quickly ramps up the pressure, requiring better unit synergy.

### Chapter 2: Sand Village
Sand Village introduces more complex map layouts with multiple paths and environmental hazards. This chapter is a significant step up in difficulty and is often where players first encounter the need for specialized ground and air units.

### Chapter 3: Marine Ford
Currently the pinnacle of the standard story mode, Marine Ford features powerful bosses and high-density waves. This is the primary farming location for endgame materials and requires a well-leveled team of Mythic units to clear consistently on higher difficulties.

## Understanding Difficulty Levels

Every stage in the **anime reversal story mode guide** can be played on three standard difficulty settings. Choosing the right difficulty is a balance between your current team strength and the specific items you need to farm.

1.  **Easy Mode:** Ideal for first-time clears and testing new unit combinations. The rewards are minimal, but it is necessary to unlock the next stages.
2.  **Hard Mode:** A moderate challenge that provides better Gem payouts and experience for your account level.
3.  **Nightmare Mode:** This is where the "good stuff" drops. If you are looking for Ammo, Curse items, or Energy Cans for evolutions, Nightmare mode is mandatory. The enemies have significantly higher health and faster movement speeds.

> **💡 Tip:** Do not rush into Nightmare mode until you have at least two Level 40+ units with decent traits. The jump in enemy HP is substantial.

## Best Units for Story Progression

To clear the campaign efficiently, you need a balanced team. While the meta shifts with new updates in 2026, certain units consistently perform well across all story chapters.

### The Hybrid King: Rio
Rio is currently one of the most valuable units for story mode because of his hybrid status. He can target both ground and air enemies, making him versatile for any map. His unique passive allows him to deal 50% more damage to enemies that fall below 30% HP, which is perfect for finishing off stubborn bosses.

### The Executioner: Amy
Amy is often used in combination with Rio. Her ability to execute enemies (including bosses) once they drop below 10% HP can save a run that is about to leak. She is widely considered "cracked" for clearing high-difficulty Nightmare stages.

### High DPS Ground Units: Shanks and Rudo
For raw damage output, Shanks and Rudo are the gold standard. They are pure DPS monsters that should be placed at "choke points" where enemies congregate. They are essential for handling the massive waves found in Marine Ford.

### Support and Economy: Speed Cart and Bachi
*   **Speed Cart:** The best money unit for early-game story mode. Placing him early allows you to afford expensive upgrades for your Mythics much faster.
*   **Bachi:** Available via the Battle Pass, Bachi acts as a powerful support unit, dealing a massive 200% damage burst every 15 attacks.

## Farming and Evolution Strategy

Progression in Anime Reversal is not just about clearing stages; it is about gathering the materials needed to evolve your units into their most powerful forms. The **anime reversal story mode guide** emphasizes the importance of the "R" NPC in the lobby for crafting.

### Evolution Materials
To evolve a unit, you typically need a specific kill count with that unit and a set of "Evil Items." These items are crafted using the following drops found in Story Mode:
*   **Trash and Metal:** Common drops from Plant Name and Sand Village.
*   **Energy Cans:** Essential for almost all mid-tier evolutions.
*   **Ammo and Curse Items:** High-tier drops primarily found in Marine Ford (Nightmare).

### Legend Stages
Once you have conquered the standard story mode, you can attempt Legend Stages. These are the hardest content currently available in 2026 and offer the rarest materials required for secret unit evolutions. Only attempt these with a full team of evolved Mythics.

## Advanced Story Tactics

As you progress through the Sand Village and Marine Ford, simple placement is no longer enough. You must consider the following tactics:

1.  **Trait Re-rolling:** Visit the upgrade section in the lobby to roll for Elite traits. Traits that provide percentage-based damage boosts are far superior to flat stat increases in late-game story mode.
2.  **The Pity System:** Remember that Mythic pity does not reset between banners. If you are struggling with a stage, spend your saved Gems on a banner featuring a unit you need; even if you don't get them immediately, you are building toward a guaranteed pull.
3.  **Event Integration:** Participate in limited-time events. Event floors often drop items like Candy Canes or special stat cards that can be equipped to your units to boost their performance in the main story chapters.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best unit for a beginner in Anime Reversal story mode?**

A: Enjo and Cha-In are excellent starter units. They are relatively easy to obtain and provide enough damage to clear the entirety of Chapter 1 on Easy and Hard modes.

**Q: How do I unlock Nightmare difficulty?**

A: You must clear the specific stage on Hard difficulty first. Once cleared, the Nightmare option will become available for that specific stage, offering significantly better loot like Energy Cans and Ammo.

**Q: Why should I focus on the Battle Pass for story mode?**

A: The Battle Pass is extremely beginner-friendly in 2026. It provides units like Bachi, who is a top-tier support, along with free Gems and XP that help you level up your account faster, unlocking more unit slots.

**Q: Where is the best place to farm Energy Cans?**

A: Nightmare mode in the Plant Name and Sand Village chapters is the most consistent way to farm Energy Cans. You can also check the Gold Merchant in the lobby, who occasionally sells them for fair prices.
